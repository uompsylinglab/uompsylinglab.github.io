Posted Feb 21, 2020

The Psycholinguistics Lab is used for several strands of linguistics research at The University of Manchester, from semantic processing studies to sociolinguistic priming and speech perception studies. Research is conducted in the lab by both students and staff alike in the department of English Language and Linguistics. You can find the Psycholinguistics Lab in the Samuel Alexander Building, NG.15 at The University of Manchester. Oxford Road, M13 9PL, Manchester, UK.

get in touch

If you need to use the lab, please check the timetable first in order to save time. If the lab is available, just send an email to the laboratory assistant Alina at alina.mclellan@postgrad.manchester.ac.uk including your desired date/s and time slot/s.

equipment

We have a diverse range of equipment and software available for use in the lab, facilitating exciting experimental linguistic research. You can book to use the lab or borrow equipment by contacting the Lab Assistant, Alina. Users of the lab may also enquire about the purchasing of new equipment needed for their research. These enquiries should be directed to the lab director Dr Vera Hohaus or the lab assistant Alina McLellan.
