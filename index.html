About Us

We are a group of psycholinguists in the Department of Linguistics and English Language at The University of Manchester. We are primarily interested in the semantic processes underlying written sentence processing - that is, the way in which written sentences are understood and the way in which meaning is derived on a word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase basis from written texts.

Our Lab Director is Vera Hohaus. Our Lab Manager is Paul Stott.

If you'd like to get in touch to discuss our research or how we might support you in your own research, please get in touch with The Lab Manager.

Our Facilities

We have state of the art equipment allowing us (and you!) to conduct:

Eye Tracking Studies

Where participants read sentences while we record time-locked eye-movement data.

Electroencephalogram (EEG) Studies

Where participants read sentences while we record time-locked brain-wave activity.

Reaction Time Studies

Where participants make decisions while we record how long it takes them to make those decisions.

Mouse Tracking Studies

Where participants make decisions while we measure the trajectory their mouse takes, and how long it takes them to get to that point, during decision-making.

Reading Time Studies

Where we measure how long it takes participants to read a given string of text.

Other Studies

We may also be able to offer support, guidance, and technical support on experimental paradigms not listed here. Please get in touch with The Lab Manager for more information and to discuss individual needs.

Training Offered

We offer training in:

  • Experimental design.
  • Use of experimental software.
  • Data tidying, wrangling, and analysis in R.
  • Type-setting in LaTeX.
  • Online data collection methods (e.g., Pavlovia, Qualtrics, Prolific).
  • Open Science Tools (e.g., OSF.io, Binder, Docker).

If you're interested in any of these training opportunities, please get in touch with The Lab Manager to discuss your training needs.
